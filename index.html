Beard Care Tips

Oil Regularly

Apply beard oil daily to keep your beard moisturized and prevent itchiness. Focus on the skin beneath your beard to prevent dryness and dandruff.

Read More

Wash Properly

Use a beard-specific shampoo 2-3 times per week. Regular shampoo can strip natural oils, leaving your beard dry and brittle.

Read More

Trim Regularly

Even if growing your beard out, regular trims help maintain shape and remove split ends. Invest in quality scissors or visit a professional.

Read More

Eat Right

A diet rich in proteins, vitamins (especially biotin), and omega-3 fatty acids promotes healthy beard growth from the inside out.

Read More

Brush Daily

Use a boar bristle brush to distribute oils evenly, exfoliate the skin, and train your beard to grow in the desired direction.

Read More

Sleep Well

Growth hormones are released during sleep. Aim for 7-8 hours per night to maximize your beard's growth potential.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I use beard oil?

For best results, apply beard oil daily, especially after showering when your pores are open and can absorb the oil more effectively. If you live in a dry climate or have particularly coarse beard hair, you may benefit from applying oil twice per day.

Learn More

What's the difference between beard oil and beard balm?

Beard oil is primarily for conditioning and moisturizing both your beard and the skin beneath. Beard balm contains similar conditioning ingredients but also includes beeswax and butter which provide light to medium hold, helping to style and tame your beard while still conditioning it.

Learn More

How can I make my beard grow faster?

While genetics largely determine your beard growth potential, you can maximize it by: maintaining a healthy diet rich in proteins and vitamins, exercising regularly, getting enough sleep, reducing stress, keeping your skin clean and exfoliated, and using quality beard products to maintain healthy hair follicles.

Learn More

How do I deal with beard itch?

Beard itch is often caused by dry skin beneath your beard. Combat it by washing with a gentle beard shampoo (not regular shampoo), applying beard oil daily to moisturize, brushing regularly to exfoliate, and drinking plenty of water. The itch typically subsides after the first few weeks of growth as your skin adjusts.

Yes! All BeardMasters products are cruelty-free and most are vegan (except for our Classic Beard Balm which contains beeswax). We never test on animals and source our ingredients ethically and sustainably.
